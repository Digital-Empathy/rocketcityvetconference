About the conference

About the conference

Who is Rocket City for?
Veterinarians who want eight hours of RACE-approved CE in a single Saturday, especially solo doctors, small-practice owners, and independent hospitals where four days away from the practice isn't realistic. Licensed vet techs, practice managers, and support staff are welcome with a ticket. The lecture content is written for veterinarians.
Where is it held?
The West End hotel in Huntsville, Alabama, inside the Bridge Street Shopping Center. The same venue is confirmed for {{year}}.
When is it?
Rocket City is a one-day conference, 8 AM to 5 PM, on a Saturday. The {{year}} date will be posted on the {{year}} conference page as soon as it's confirmed.
Is the CE recognized in my state?
Yes. Lectures are RACE-approved, which is the national standard for veterinary continuing education. Out-of-state attendees can earn credit that counts in their home state.
Is there a virtual option?
No. Alabama's continuing education requirement specifies in-person attendance, and Rocket City is built to honor that.
Attending

Attending

Can I bring my staff?
Yes. Licensed vet techs, practice managers, and support staff are welcome with their own ticket. The lecture content is written for veterinarians, but LVTs in Alabama will earn all eight hours of their annual CE requirement in one Saturday.
Can I bring my kids?
Rocket City does not provide childcare. Babies are welcome with their parent. Older children may attend with their own ticket. Exact age policy and pricing for minors will be confirmed before the {{year}} event opens.
Is there a place to nurse or pump?
Yes. A private mother's room is available throughout the day, with a fridge for milk storage. Bringing your baby is fine.
What's included in the ticket?
Eight hours of CE, hot breakfast, hot lunch, an afternoon snack, coffee and water all day, the full conference swag bag, and the optional end-of-day social with food and drinks.
Will food cover dietary restrictions?
Yes. Allergy and dietary information is collected at registration. Menus are planned around what attendees can eat, not around what's easy to serve.
Where should I stay?
The West End is a hotel. Booking a room at the venue means you take the elevator to the conference instead of driving to it. Out-of-towners can arrive Friday, attend Saturday, and head out Sunday morning without leaving the building.
Is there parking at the venue?
Parking details and any guest discount will be confirmed before {{year}} registration opens.
What's the refund or transfer policy?
Refund and transfer policy will be confirmed before {{year}} registration opens.

Vendors and sponsors

Who is in the vendor hall?
Independent veterinary equipment, tool, and technology companies who want to support independent practices.
Why are corporate chains and recruiters not invited?
Because the rest of the room is made up of doctors and staff from independent hospitals, and Rocket City is built to protect them. The vendor hall is a place to learn about new equipment and tools, not a place to lose your associate to a recruiter at lunch.
