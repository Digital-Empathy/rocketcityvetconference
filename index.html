Why this conference exists

She didn't set out to start a conference. She set out to fix one.

A few years ago, {{founderName}}, a solo veterinarian and practice owner with a six-month-old nursing baby, took her infant and her mother across state lines to fulfill Alabama's in-person CE requirement, which makes no exception for childbirth. She spent the conference running between the lecture hall and her hotel room, retained almost nothing, and came home convinced something had to change.

Huntsville is the largest city in Alabama. It didn't have a major veterinary conference. At the next Madison County VMA meeting, {{founderFirst}} asked why. There wasn't a good answer. By the time she sat down, she'd accidentally founded Rocket City.

Year one was eight hours of CE and seven vendors. Year four was twenty-one vendors and a full house at the West End hotel. Every year since, the conference has been built around one question:

What would CE look like if it were built for the people who can’t disappear for a week?

Rocket City is built for the veterinarians most conferences quietly aren't designed for: solo doctors, small-practice owners, and anyone running an independent hospital where four days away means the practice closes, or means paying a relief vet to see a fraction of your normal caseload.

It's also built around a vendor hall that won't recruit your staff out from under you. Corporate chains and recruiting firms aren't invited. The vendors at Rocket City are there to show you new equipment, new tools, and new technology, not to walk away with your associate at lunch.

+ Bring your team.  Licensed vet techs, practice managers, and support staff are welcome. Because lectures are RACE-approved at the doctor level, your LVTs will earn all eight hours of their annual Alabama CE requirement in one Saturday. The content itself is written for veterinarians. Come knowing that, and the day works for everyone.

The hundred small things

All the things you didn’t have to ask for.

Most conferences are uncomfortable not because of any one thing, but because of every small thing nobody bothered to think about. The chairs that aren't at tables. The lunch that comes in a box. The bathroom that's just a bathroom. The mother who has nowhere to nurse.

Rocket City starts from the opposite belief: that comfort is the sum of decisions, and a hundred small ones, made on purpose, are the entire difference between a day you endure and a day you'd actually do again.

  1. Seating

    Real chairs at real tables.

    Not chairs in a row with a notepad in your lap. Eight hours is a long time to sit.

  2. The Meal

    Hot lunch, never a box.

    Real meals, planned around dietary needs you don't have to remind anyone about.

  3. Swag

    A swag bag worth taking home.

    A handpicked-soft t-shirt with "you're worth it" on the back, an encouragement notebook, antibac that comes with a note, and a soy candle with a label only a veterinarian will laugh at. The most recent one was "smells better than the next dumpster fire."

  4. Care Stations

    Care stations in the bathrooms.

    Deodorant, dental floss, lint roller, Tide wipes, mints, body spray. The sign reads: you deserve a stress-free day.

  5. For Parents

    A private mother’s room.

    With a fridge for milk storage. The alternative is exactly what got Rocket City started in the first place.

  6. All Day

    Breakfast, lunch, snacks, coffee, water all day.

    Bring nothing but yourself. Plus an optional end-of-day social with food and drinks if you want to stay.
